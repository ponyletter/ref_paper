Design of agricultural product 
traceability system based on 
blockchain and RFID
Li Li1,2, Pengbo Tian1,2, Jiapeng Dai1 & Fengjuan Miao1
Ensuring the traceability of agricultural products is essential for quality control and food safety. Recent 
technological advances have provided new ways to enhance traceability systems. This study aims to 
use blockchain technology, centralized database and RFID tags to develop a secure agricultural product 
traceability system, retain the detailed information of agricultural products traceability, ensure that 
the summary information of agricultural products on the chain cannot be modified, and optimize 
the SM3 algorithm to effectively summarize the traceability data and improve the efficiency of the 
system. The aggregated data is time-stamped, recorded on the blockchain, and written into an RFID 
tag. The optimization of the SM3 algorithm improved the efficiency by 30% and reduced the execution 
time of 192-byte messages to 210µs. The system ensures accurate linking of traceability data through 
secure data retention and unalterable summaries on the blockchain. The integrated use of blockchain, 
centralized database and RFID technology, as well as the enhanced SM3 algorithm, allows the system 
to meet the standards for data accuracy and performance requirements in agricultural traceability 
applications.
Keywords Traceability of agricultural products, RFID, Blockchain, SM3, HSM,  Internet of things 
technology
In recent years, with the advancement of the Internet of Things (IoT) industry, the application of sensors and big 
data for data collection and storage has significantly enhanced China’s traceability industry. However, a series 
of issues persist. Traditional anti-counterfeiting traceability technologies, such as QR codes and fluorescence 
markers, remain relatively outdated. These methods are prone to forgery and fail to provide detailed traceability 
information, limiting their effectiveness in ensuring product safety and transparency1–3. Therefore, the traditional 
agricultural product traceability data system has barriers to interaction, limited interaction capabilities, and is 
prone to information islands4,5.
Currently, the majority of applications utilizing RFID-based traceability systems are primarily designed for 
single-node traceability, with a predominant focus on tracking information at the planting stage. Consequently, 
the management of comprehensive traceability data, including information from subsequent stages of the 
agricultural supply chain, remains underdeveloped. This lack of integration limits the effectiveness of these 
systems in providing a holistic view of agricultural product traceability and addressing the entire lifecycle 
of the product6,7. Agricultural products involve multiple links in the circulation process, and it is difficult to 
unify the synchronization of traceability information based solely on RFID tags, resulting in low accuracy of 
traceability data and errors that make it difficult to ensure the accuracy of traceability data8–10. Traceability 
data is stored in a central database, and if the database is subjected to tools, the data can be easily leaked and 
tampered with11–13. RFID-based traceability systems that rely on centralized databases face significant challenges 
in ensuring the authenticity and integrity of traceability data. Additionally, there is insufficient emphasis on 
the security risks associated with data interactions between readers and databases. The potential vulnerabilities 
in reader devices expose the system to security threats and attacks, compromising the overall reliability and 
protection of the traceability information14,15. Simultaneously, the inherent characteristics of blockchain 
technology—decentralization, immutability, traceability, and trust—make it exceptionally well-suited to address 
the challenges associated with centralized management and multi-party trust issues prevalent in current RFID 
anti-counterfeiting traceability systems. Blockchain’s distributed ledger capabilities offer a robust solution 
for enhancing data integrity, security, and transparency, effectively mitigating the limitations of traditional 
centralized traceability approaches16–18. However, neither personal research nor the blockchain platform can 
ensure the authenticity of the traceability data before it is put on the chain, and when the amount of traceability

Integrating traditional agricultural product traceability systems with RFID and blockchain technologies offers 
a transformative enhancement in transparency and data integrity. The synergy of blockchain’s decentralized 
ledger and RFID’s real-time tracking capabilities significantly bolsters consumer trust and satisfaction 
by ensuring the authenticity and reliability of traceability data. This integration not only fosters sustainable 
development and scalability in industry transformation but also elevates the overall operational efficiency of 
commodity circulation. As a result, it enhances the social value of agricultural products and presents substantial 
potential for advancement and application within engineering practices.
Key technologies
Blockchain technology
Blockchain technology is a decentralized distributed digital ledger used to record transactions and data in a 
secure and transparent manner. It employs encryption algorithms to ensure the integrity and validity of the 
data stored on it, and once the data is recorded on it, it is nearly impossible to change, hack, or manipulate. 
Blockchain technology underpins cryptocurrencies such as Bitcoin and Ethereum, but its potential applications 
extend beyond digital currencies. It can be Bitcoin and Ethereum, among others, but its potential applications 
extend beyond digital currencies. It has been used in supply chain management, identity verification, voting 
systems, and more.
This paper uses the storage method of combining blockchain and centralized database (MySql), which only 
stores the traceability summary data of agricultural products calculated by SM3, which reduces the storage space 
of the blockchain system and improves the query and search speed. The centralized database (MySql) is used to 
store the traceability details of agricultural products, and RFID tags are used to record the summary information 
of agricultural products at each stage.
RFID sensing technology
RFID technology allows the antenna to play its important role, it can cross-couple the energy of electromagnetic 
waves and other physical factors, so that the antenna can exert its maximum efficiency. In addition, the field area

antenna more stable, so as to better meet the requirements of RFID technology. According to this principle, the 
information transmission between the reader and the tag can be divided into two types: backscatter modulation 
and load modulation. In the passive RFID system, the reader receives the data sent by the electronic tag, and the 
communication method used is backscatter modulation. The traditional RFID tag consists of an antenna and 
a chip, and the input impedance of the chip itself needs to be adjusted by adding an impedance switch, and the 
schematic diagram is shown in Fig. 1.

mobile phone. By applying Maxwell’s equations, an accurate near-field and far-field model of the antenna 
radiation field can be established, which is helpful to better understand the application mechanism of RFID

Data summary information generation and validation
In the process of data acquisition and storage, the domestic SM3 algorithm is used to perform summary 
calculation on the original data, and the original data and summary data are further processed through the data 
processing layer. After completing the process of each node, the summary data of this node is summarized, and 
the summary data of the SM3 algorithm of the sum is calculated and written into the RFID tag. The process of 
calculating data using the SM3 algorithm summary is as follows: According to the standard guidance of the State 
Cryptography Administration, the SM3 algorithm specifies that the length of the input data is less than 264 bits 
in the process of calculation. In the traceability data of agricultural products, through the pre-setting, the data of 
each data summary operation is much less than 264 bits, and the operation steps by feeding the original data into 
the SM3 algorithm are as follows: (1) Message length filling; (2) Message expansion; (3) Iterative compression; 
(4) Summary data outputs.
Information storage and verification
In the process of traceability of agricultural products, RFID tags are pasted inside the commodity packaging. 
According to the flow characteristics in the process of traceability of agricultural products, the labels selected 
by this system are ultra-high frequency labels with low cost, high reliability, strong anti-interference and long 
radiation distance.
In the RFID tag, the storage area is composed of four areas, namely: tag identification number, electronic 
product code, user data storage area and reserved area, the four areas are independent of each other, data storage 
can be carried out, and the tag function area is divided as shown in Table 2. The reserved area is mainly used for user 
storage access passwords, kill passwords, and tags are password verified and locked in the process of interacting 
with the reader, further improving the security of system identification. Agricultural products involve a variety 
of categories in the circulation process, and the coding and classification of items can be realized through the 
identification inside the label. Starting from 20 H as the address, considering the number of types of agricultural 
products, 12 bytes are selected for coding classification, and the final coding range is: 00000001 ~ FFFFFFFFF. 
By coding agricultural products to facilitate post-management and query, it is conducive to faster identification, 
warehousing, distribution and other functions of commodities in the later stage. The tag identification area is 
a globally unique code that cannot be changed by the user. In the process of data storage, the TID value of the 
RFID tag pasted on the surface of each agricultural product is used as the index value of the MySql database, 
and the user area can customize the data by the user. The output value combined with the SM3 algorithm in this 
scheme is fixed at 32 bytes and 256 bits, and the user storage area of the tag is 512 bits by looking up the data 
sheet, which meets the requirements of the agricultural product traceability system.

Sm3 algorithm improvement and optimization
By theoretically analyzing the execution time of the algorithm and the running time of the ISYSTEM debugger 
tracking function, the time data monitored by the debugger is analyzed and it is concluded that during the 
execution of the SM3 algorithm, the time consumption of the compression function accounts for 58.9%, and 
the time consumption of message expansion reaches 25.4%, as shown in Fig. 8a, the key to improving the fast 
implementation of the SM3 algorithm is to reduce the execution time of the compression function and message 
expansion.
The purpose of the message expansion phase is to divide 512-bit messages into two groups. The first group is 
given 68 words and is denoted as w_0-w_67, and the second group is 64 characters, denoted as w_0^’-w_63^’. 
In the process of operating 132 words, the call and storage time of data can be reduced, and the execution time 
of the algorithm can be reduced indirectly. Reduce the loading of unnecessary messages by adjusting the initial 
value calculation of the compression function. Increase the speed of computation during execution by reducing 
w_0^’-w_63^’ computation and storage.

Written in C language, the SM3 algorithm, SHA-256 algorithm, and optimized SM3 algorithm are

ESP8266 can be run as a host alone, independently through SDK development, or as a slave device driven 
by other MCUs. When ESP8266 is running as the main control module, the application can be downloaded to

supports the operation of an embedded operating system with integrated Wi-Fi, MAC, BB, RF, PA, LNA, and 
on-board antennas. The appearance and circuit diagram of the ESP8266 module are shown in Fig. 11b.
In Infineon’s AURIX microcontroller, its internal HSM core meets the Medium Version level, with TriCore 
as the master core and HSM as the slave core. Work is carried out when the SM3 algorithm is called, and the call 
can be made by bridge connection between the master and slave cores. Among them, the ARM core in HSM 
contains 24-bit timers, 8 interrupt nodes, 8 priorities, and has a memory protection unit (MPU). The Boot ROM 
is 4 KB and contains the code and read-only data necessary to start the HSM, and this ROM can be shut down 
after booting through DBGCTR, and each operation of the boot ROM will be checked and tested. The local 
RAM size is 96 KB, and no data is retained after the off-chain operation. The internal watchdog circuit is a 16-bit 
timer, where Flash is 40 × 16 KB = 640 KB. TP and HSM operations are used during interaction with the main 
core, requiring operations from PF0 SO to S39, as shown in Fig. 12.
Data testing and processing
This paper uses blockchain, RFID tags, and centralized databases to ensure the authenticity of agricultural 
product traceability. By using a virtual machine to build a server system and using Windows Server 2016 
operating system, create 5 nodes to build a blockchain network to store summary information of agricultural 
product traceability; Use a single host to deploy the business system, and install MySql 8.0 to store detailed data 
of agricultural product traceability. The configuration information is shown in Table 3.
The system’s blockchain platform is designed to be based on Hyperledger Fabric and can be developed 
using general-purpose programming languages such as Java, GO, and Node.js, rather than being limited to the 
platform’s own specific language (DSL). Fabric is a consortium chain, so only users who agree to the organization 
can join the network, and the participants in Fabric are aware of each other. The development of Fabirc mainly 
includes network construction, smart contract writing, and SDK calling smart contracts.
The user’s operation interface is used for data communication with RFID readers and blockchain platforms, 
mainly including identity registration, product registration, product sending, product receiving and product 
inquiry. When the user interface receives the identity registration request and fingerprint information sent by 
the reader, enter the ID and name through the interactive interface, click Register, the underlying logic will 
write the data into the HTTP request URL parameter, and write the registration information to the blockchain 
platform by sending POST request submission parameters to the blockchain platform. When the user reads the 
RFID tag through the reader, the data of product registration, product sending and product reception can be 
sent through the reader. The data is parsed and displayed on the user interface. After the user enters the product 
ID and clicks the query, submits parameters to the blockchain platform by sending an HTTP POST request, and 
obtains the query result. The flow of the user interaction module is shown in Fig. 13.
The web server adopts the GO language, based on the BeeGO framework for development, processing HTTP 
requests sent by the user operation interface, database calls and calling the blockchain GO SDK to achieve 
interaction with the blockchain, the process design of beego is shown in Fig. 14. After receiving the HTTP 
request, the web server mainly does the following operations: (1) Parameter processing: obtain the parameters 
through GetString (parameter name), and determine whether the parameters are empty or valid, and proceed 
to the next step if they are all composite. (2) Blockchain interaction: Query the blockchain status and update the 
status by calling the GO SDK, and return the call result to the user. (3) Database update: There is a local SQLite 
database in the server, and when the interaction with the blockchain is successful, the data can be written to the 
centralized database to facilitate future data queries. (4) Return user information: define a Respons structure, 
which contains State and Result. State is the response of the user’s request that succeeded or failed, and is 1 if 
successful, otherwise 0.

the central database. Through the transaction address of the blockchain, read the data summary information
